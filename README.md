# Long Duration Earnings Analysis
### A Hedge-Adjusted Total Return Framework for Corporate Earnings Analysis

A research presentation exploring the idea that the market's response to earnings is transmitted through a company's cost of capital rather than through reported earnings metrics alone.

Instead of treating earnings as an isolated accounting event, this framework models earnings as a financing event whose effects propagate through the firm's corporate debt. By observing hedge-adjusted Total Return (TR) on long-duration corporate bonds relative to comparable credit, the presentation proposes a practical relative value framework for anticipating equity reactions to earnings. :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}

---

## Overview

Traditional earnings analysis tends to revolve around metrics such as:

- EPS
- Revenue surprises
- Guidance
- Margin expansion
- Analyst expectations

While useful, these statistics are largely post-hoc observations.

This presentation argues that the more meaningful signal is the change in the economics of owning the firm's debt.

If carrying the company's bonds becomes materially more or less attractive after accounting for financing and hedging costs, that change represents a genuine shift in balance-sheet quality that equity markets eventually reflect.

---

## Core Thesis

Rather than asking:

> "Were earnings good?"

ask

> "Did the firm's cost of capital improve or deteriorate?"

Corporate debt is treated as the transmission mechanism between accounting information and market pricing.

---

## Framework

Instead of using yield alone:

**Hedge Adjusted Total Return**

```
TR =
Yield
− Bid/Ask Cost
− Duration Hedge Cost
− Credit Hedge Cost
− Special Repo
```

Yield is only one component of the economics of holding corporate debt.

A proper total return measure should include:

- financing costs
- execution costs
- hedge costs
- funding considerations

---

## Relative Value Methodology

Individual bond TR is not interpreted in isolation.

Instead, compare it against a comparable credit index.

Example:

BBB+ Corporate Bond

vs.

BBB+ Corporate Credit Index

Where possible:

- live bond basket
- live repo
- live hedge costs
- live CDX

For desks without institutional infrastructure, a practical approximation can be constructed using:

- SOFR
- CDX
- Duration hedge approximation
- Average repo

which produces a surprisingly effective proxy for relative value analysis.

---

## Why Long Duration?

The presentation intentionally uses the longest-duration corporate bond available.

Reasons include:

- Highest sensitivity to financing conditions
- Highest duration exposure
- Largest response to liquidity changes
- Strong exposure to macro expectations
- Largest aggregate cashflow sensitivity

Rather than viewing this sensitivity as undesirable noise, it becomes the signal.

Long-duration debt amplifies changes in market perception surrounding corporate balance sheets.

---

## Earnings Philosophy

During earnings season:

- analysts disagree
- narratives conflict
- headlines compete
- options positioning dominates discussion

Debt behaves differently.

Corporate bond total return represents the realized economics of financing the company.

This framework therefore attempts to forecast:

- the market's response to earnings

rather than

- earnings themselves.
